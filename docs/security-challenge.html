Patterns & Insights

🧠
Social Engineering Dominated
With a 30% success rate, social engineering was by far the most effective category. Human persuasion techniques proved more powerful than any technical exploit.
🔒
Encoding = Zero Breakthroughs
Despite 8 different encoding techniques (Base64, ROT13, hex, Unicode, gematria, binary, emoji, multi-layer), none achieved even partial success.
🎨
Creativity Scaled Over Time
Attacks evolved from simple encoding in Day 1 to sophisticated multi-technique hybrids by Day 8. Players learned from each other and iterated rapidly.
👥
Coordination Amplified Impact
The most effective attacks involved multiple players working together. Group pressure simulation achieved partial success through coordinated social engineering.
